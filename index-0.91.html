Pediatric Chronic Pain Clinics

The "Pain Team" Prepared

Your pediatric pain clinic will have various health care professionals to give control back to you from pain. A typical team may contain professionals such as these:

Nurse-Practitioner

Nurse practitioners provide direct care, focusing on health promotion and the treatment and management of health conditions.

They have an expanded scope of practice and can diagnose, order, and interpret diagnostic tests. They can also prescribe medications and perform certain procedures.

Physiotherapist

Physiotherapists are experts on how the body moves and what stops it from moving.

As movement specialists, physiotherapists will use a hands-on approach to help you restore, maximize, and maintain movement for the rest of your life.

Psychologist

A psychologist studies, from a scientific viewpoint, how we think, feel, and behave, and applies this knowledge to help people understand, explain, and change their behavior.

"Chronic pain is a disease. Our job is to help you manage it."

The Pain Team will strive to help you live a better life than you have since the pain started.

Whatever life goals you may have - whether it be sports, studying, or working, the team will help you meet those goals.

Let's Strategize About

How to Combat Pain Signals

Too little or too much physical activity, slepp problems, stress, anxiety, and sadness can all affect the pain signals going to the brain. Here are some ideas to come up with strategies in fighting pain signals.

Relaxation / Chilling Out

We all need to chil-lax some times, and its good to take some break when the pain is getting out of hand (isSampleText).

Anything from listening to music to stretching out on the couch to distract the pain can be helpful (isSampleText).

Gentle Exercise

Gentle exercises can motivate your body and mind to be a bit more active and prevent being overly fatigued (isSampleText).

Taking a jog, doing yoga, playing badminton, or even mindful breathing exercises can all help (isSampleText).

Plenty of Hydration

Your body needs plenty of water to function properly. Drinking once in a while to prevent becoming thirsty is often a good idea (isSampleText).

Water is often the best drink for staying hydrated as opposed to drinking pop or other soft drinks (isSampleText).

"No Pain No Gain? Nah - Know Pain Know Gain!"

Whatever goal that needs to be met - rockclimbing a mountain, earning a certificate, performing at a concert, know your limits.

Stopping something because of painful symptom is not a sign of weakness. Not overextending your body and controlling your pain is one of real signs of strength and fortitude.
